# Jai Jesus Christ – Miracles of the Messiah (Miracles, Deliverances, and Healings Performed by Lord Jesus Christ)

The New Testament Gospels record numerous accounts of supernatural acts by Lord Jesus Christ that demonstrated His divine authority, compassion, and power over creation, disease, and evil forces.

## Miracles
These encompass acts of power over natural elements, provision, and the raising of the dead.

Turning water into wine at the wedding in Cana, demonstrating mastery over creation (John 2:1-11).
Miraculous catch of fish on the Lake of Gennesaret, revealing authority over the natural world (Luke 5:1-11).
Calming a violent storm on the Sea of Galilee with a word (Matthew 8:23-27; Mark 4:35-41; Luke 8:22-25).
Feeding approximately 5,000 men (plus women and children) with five loaves and two fish (Matthew 14:13-21; Mark 6:30-44; Luke 9:10-17; John 6:1-15).
Walking on the water of the Sea of Galilee (Matthew 14:22-33; Mark 6:45-52; John 6:16-21).
Feeding approximately 4,000 men (plus women and children) with seven loaves and a few fish (Matthew 15:32-39; Mark 8:1-10).
Providing a coin in a fish’s mouth to pay the temple tax (Matthew 17:24-27).
Cursing a barren fig tree, causing it to wither instantly (Matthew 21:18-22; Mark 11:12-25).
Raising the widow’s son from the dead in Nain (Luke 7:11-17).
Raising Jairus’ daughter from the dead (Matthew 9:18-26; Mark 5:21-43; Luke 8:40-56).
Raising Lazarus from the dead after four days in the tomb (John 11:1-44).
Second miraculous catch of 153 large fish after the resurrection (John 21:1-11).

## Deliverances
These involve the expulsion of demons or unclean spirits, affirming Jesus’ authority over spiritual forces.

Casting out an unclean spirit from a man in the synagogue at Capernaum (Mark 1:23-28; Luke 4:33-37).
Delivering the Gerasene (or Gadarenes) demoniac, who was possessed by a legion of spirits, sending them into a herd of pigs (Matthew 8:28-34; Mark 5:1-20; Luke 8:26-39).
Delivering the daughter of a Canaanite (Syro-Phoenician) woman from demon possession at a distance (Matthew 15:21-28; Mark 7:24-30).
Casting out a demon causing muteness (Matthew 9:32-34).
Casting out a demon from a man who was both blind and mute (Matthew 12:22-23; Luke 11:14).
Casting out a demon from a boy suffering from seizures and self-harm (Matthew 17:14-21; Mark 9:14-29; Luke 9:37-43).

## Healings
These focus on the restoration of physical ailments, infirmities, and conditions unrelated to explicit demonic possession.

Cleansing a man with leprosy (Matthew 8:1-4; Mark 1:40-45; Luke 5:12-16).
Healing the centurion’s paralyzed servant at a distance (Matthew 8:5-13; Luke 7:1-10).
Healing Peter’s mother-in-law of a high fever (Matthew 8:14-15; Mark 1:29-31; Luke 4:38-39).
Healing a paralytic lowered through the roof, after forgiving his sins (Matthew 9:1-8; Mark 2:1-12; Luke 5:17-26).
Restoring a man’s withered hand on the Sabbath (Matthew 12:9-14; Mark 3:1-6; Luke 6:6-11).
Healing a woman who had suffered from bleeding for twelve years (Matthew 9:20-22; Mark 5:25-34; Luke 8:43-48).
Restoring sight to two blind men (Matthew 9:27-31).
Healing an invalid of thirty-eight years at the Pool of Bethesda (John 5:1-15).
Healing the royal official’s son of a life-threatening illness at a distance (John 4:46-54).
Healing a man born blind by applying mud and instructing him to wash (John 9:1-12).
Healing a deaf man with a speech impediment (Mark 7:31-37).
Restoring sight to blind Bartimaeus near Jericho (Mark 10:46-52; Luke 18:35-43).
Cleansing ten men with leprosy, with only one returning to give thanks (Luke 17:11-19).
Healing a woman crippled and bent over for eighteen years (Luke 13:10-17).
Healing a man with dropsy (edema) on the Sabbath (Luke 14:1-6).
Restoring the severed ear of the high priest’s servant, Malchus (Luke 22:50-51).
